abstract: >
  We study whether ordering RLHF training examples by an estimated
  difficulty score improves sample efficiency and final reward-model
  agreement compared to random shuffling. Preliminary results on a
  mid-scale instruction-tuned model show faster early-training reward
  gains, with the advantage shrinking as training progresses.
