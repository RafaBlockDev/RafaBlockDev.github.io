Quick pointers, mostly for my own future reference:

- **Needle-in-a-haystack** style probes are useful but saturate fast and
  don't capture multi-hop retrieval.
- Benchmarks that require *aggregating* evidence across the context
  (not just locating one fact) are much better stress tests for
  sparsity-based methods, since they punish over-aggressive pruning.
- Perplexity alone is a weak proxy once context length passes ~32k
  tokens — worth pairing with a task-level metric.
